Cultural and historical values in Sri Lanka

Due to the vast history of rulers, varying degrees of colonial impact, variety of religions and modernixing influences, Sri Lanka has gained a very diversed culture.Over 75% of population are Buddhists while others are Sri Lankan Tamils,Muslims, and Catholics.Buddhist temples, Hindu kovils, mosques and churches have their own colourful rituals which comprises the most visible featues of the cultural landscape in Sri Lanka.
In addition, the hostorical locations, that have been preserved for years clearly illustrate the technological advancement gained by the ancestors of Sri Lanka.Most of those ancient ruins are located at the ancient city of Anuradhapura, which has a written history that runs up to 5th century bc.Anuradhapura is the first kingdom in Sri Lanka, and after Anuradhapura, kingdoms were taken to Polonnaruwa, Dambadeniya, Yapahuwa, Kurunegala, and finally, before the british colonization, Kandy.All of these locations have their own historic values because of the effects of various cultures.
For instance, Polonnaruwa has historical ruins with a taste of tamil culture, because of the invasion happened in Polonnaruwa era, by the tamil king named Kalinga Magha, and in Kandy, there is an effect of british architectures in some buildings, due to the effects of british emperor.

Most of people find it interesting, exploring those historical ruins, because there is much to explore and much to learn.
