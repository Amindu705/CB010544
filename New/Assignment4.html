Main Attractions

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- There are a lot of places in Sri Lanka that are famous among both local and foriegn tourists.Waterfalls that falls through the mountains, stunning beaches, heritage locations, wildlife to explore, and the variety of cultures attract more people to visit Sri Lanka.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

01)Waterfalls

There are more than 100 waterfalls in Sri Lanka, and some of them are not yet identified, due to the remote locations and not making famous among public.The strong gushes of water that falls from high altitudes will surely give you goosebumps, but you can't deny the beauty of them. These are some of the most famous waterfalls in Sri Lanka

01)Bakers falls

Bakers falls is an eye catching waterfall, which falls from about 20m height, was named after it's founder, Sir Samuel Bakers.When he was going for hunting in Horton plains(which is also an interesting place that you can go hiking click here to find more.) You can have a chilling experience amidst purple rhodendrom flowers and lush green ferns.But if you are travelling in rainy season, better to avoid getting too much closer to this beauty, because sudden water level rise can cause drowning, and also, the rock surfaces can get slippery.Always be causious when you are travelling :)

02)Dunhida Falls

There is no way you can miss Dunhida while talking about most eyecatching waterfalls in Sri Lanka.This waterfall is formed by the diversion of Badulla river. This 65m beauty is easily reachable, and it is a 5min drive from Badulla town.The smoky nature of the environment caused by the splashing water, adds some extra beauty to this falls, making it more famous among both locals and foriegners.Though it is easily accessable, it is better if you wear a pair of shoes, or a footwear that covers your feet, because there are leaches in the walking path. Also, it would be better if you hire a local guide, because some proper experience would be always better.

03)Bambarakanda/Horse-tail falls

Bambarakanda falls, which is also known as the Horse-tail falls, is the tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka(265m)Surrounded by a densed pine forest, and formed ny the one of walawe river's tributaries. Most people find it relaxing because of the location and the quality of the environment.Having a quick bath in this waterfall is not recommended, because there are previous records of deaths who attempted to have a bath in this waterfall.But you can defenitely enjoy by watching it in a safe distance, as others do.

02)Places for Hiking and Camping

Most of people find it interesting to hike in a safe and beautiful place.Of course there are many places that you can enjoy hiking and camping.If you love hiking, these are some places that you can go in Sri Lanka.

01)Horton Plains

One of the most beautiful World Heritage Sites in the world is definitely the Horton Plains National Park in Sri Lanka. The park is spread over the two highest mountains in the area called Kirigalpotta and Totapola. The plateau-like structure along with plains on one end, thick forests and mountains on other make the Central Highlands of Sri Lanka. You will find a number of waterfalls, lakes, rocky hills and views of the ocean in this part of the country. The forest is home to a number of wild animals and birds, however, most large animals remain elusive and unapproachable.
The best time to visit the park is the start of the year, especially from January to March. The Horton Plains National Park weather is quite cloudy and misty around the year, however, the beginning of the year has less precipitation which leads to better views. You would also come across lesser crowds. It is also best to start early for your journey to the park and reach the World’s End by nine in the morning to ensure a good view.

02)Ella

Ella Rock is located in Ella, a town high in the mountainous region of Sri Lanka. Standing at an elevation of 1,041 metres above sea level, Ella Town is located right in the centre of the island, and you can start the Ella Rock hike directly from the main high street.Many people choose to start the Ella Rock hike early in the morning, and it is indeed the best option.
